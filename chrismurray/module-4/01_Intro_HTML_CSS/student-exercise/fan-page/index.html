Team History

The Detroit Red Wings are a professional ice hockey team based in Detroit, Michigan. They are a member of the Atlantic Division of the Eastern Conference in the National Hockey League. The Red Wings were founded in 1926 and are one of the Original Six teams of the league. As of 2020 the Red Wings have won the most Stanley Cup championships of any US based NHL franchise with 11. From 1983-2016 the Red Wings made the playoffs 30 times in 32 seasons, including 25 straight from 1990-2016. This is tied for the third longest streak in NHL history.

Octopus Tradition

The Legend of the Octopus is a sporting tradition during playoff games, where fans throw octopus onto the ice for good luck. This tradition started in 1952 when Peter Cusimano, local fish market owner, threw one from the stands onto the ice. The eight tentacles on the octopus was meant to symbolize the eight wins it took to win the Stanley Cup Championship. The Red Wings went on to win the cup that year and it has been a good luck charm ever since. It has been banned by the NHL, but the tradition lives on.
